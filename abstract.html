ABSTRACT

 Health insurance is a critical component of modern healthcare systems, providing financial protection against medical expenses and ensuring access to essential healthcare services. It operates through various models, including employer sponsored plans, private insurance, and government-funded programs like Medicaid and Medicare. The affordability, accessibility, and effectiveness of health insurance depend on several factors, such as demographics, income levels, employment status, and healthcare policies. Despite its importance, challenges persist, including high premiums, out-of-pocket costs, claim denials, and disparities in coverage among different socioeconomic groups. Many individuals struggle with understanding their insurance benefits, leading to underutilization of preventive care services. Moreover, healthcare reforms and technological advancements continue to shape the landscape of health insurance, influencing its efficiency and inclusivity. This study explores the key aspects of health insurance, including coverage options, cost implications, consumer satisfaction, and areas for improvement. Understanding these elements is crucial for policymakers, insurers, and individuals to enhance the accessibility and affordability of healthcare services while ensuring financial sustainability.
